Career

I am a PhD candidate at the Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior, Konstanz, Germany. I am strongly inclined towards research that demand combined computational, theoretical, and data-driven approaches. My PhD focuses on behavioural influence in social groups at different time-scales, and I address these questions theoretically, as well as using data from different model organisms.

My Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Biology were awarded in 2020 by the Indian Institute of Science. All my pre-PhD research was in collective behaviour and animal movement, carried out in affiliation with various groups at the Centre for Ecological Sciences. During this time, I worked on four highly quantitative projects. I have also taken numerous courses relevant to quantitative biology, and was a Teaching Assistant for the course Quantitative Ecology: Research Design and Inference at CES in 2019.

My CV provides more details about my academic life.

Life

I am extremely interested in reading really old classic books and papers in various fields, to get a picture of science and society decades or centuries ago. Firmly adherent to the fact that science does not happen in a vaccuum, and in the (probably naive) hope that things can be better, I am a vocal and harsh critic of academia. I am also committed to free and open source software, and attempt to use only such software in my life and research.

On a more light-hearted note, I am also an avid fan of all types of classical music, including but not limited to western, carnatic, and islamic. I have made attempts at playing the flute, and drawing whiteboard art, and am passably good at them. I write both humorous as well as serious satire for various issues. I have established and organised several academic and non-academic events, some more successful than others. These include the lecture series Umwelten IISc, the UG Theoretical Biology Circle, and the CES Shut-Up-and-Write circle, among several others

Funding

I am grateful to have been a fellow of the Indian Government's Kishore Vaigyanik Protsahan Yojana, which gave me financial freedom at a very early age. I strongly recommend applying to this scholarhip if you're an Indian highschooler. The Indian Government has recently closed this fellowship in what can only be described, in polite terms, as a puzzling move. I stand by the thousands of former awardees of this fellowship who have excoriated this decision.

I am currently supported by the DAAD Graduate Student Scholarship Programme.
