Behavioural algorithms

All animals behave. Dynamically switching between a set of behavioural states allows animals to thrive in heterogeneous and stochastically varying environments. I analyse animal behavioural sequence data to tell what cross-species similarities and differences exist in the algorithms by which animals execute behaviour.

For studying behavioural sequences, I use approaches from survival analysis, information theory, and other useful transdisciplinary methods. To infer the behavioural sequences in the first place, I use accelerometers, and part of my research also focusses on methods involved in accelerometric behaviour recognition.

Group dynamics

Interactions at multiple temporal and conceptual scales can influence the movement and behaviour of animals in groups. I use individual-based simulations and biologger data to study how, using their advanced cognitive capabilities, animals make collective behavioural decisions.

Other curiosities

At a wide variety of time-scales, I ask questions regarding specific behaviours in specific species. These range from 24 h daily activity patterns and their social synchronisation in spotted hyenas to instant-to-instant vigilance decisions in groups of meerkats.
