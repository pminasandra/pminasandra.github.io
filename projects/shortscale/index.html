The mechanisms and consequences of behavioural influence

In a group of interacting animals, individuals likely influence each others' behaviours. The question of who influences whom has been a classic question in the study of behaviour that still remains largely unexplored. In this project, I will be looking at behavioural data from cichlids and meerkats to answer the following questions:

  1. At the group scale, what factors determine the influence of an individual? Size, location, dominance?
  2. At the individual scale, does an individual's behaviour become more 'jittery' if they are less influential/dominant?
  3. At the bout scale, to what extent is influence encoded the states vs the transition-in-state events?
